Self-confidence and self-control

The research results convincingly demonstrate that the most constant factor distinguishing successful and unsuccessful athletes is self-confidence. This means that the strongest athletes, regardless of the sport, constantly demonstrate a high degree of confidence in themselves and in their abilities.

Tips to become self-confident

  • Tip 1. Stop feeling confident and start thinking confidently.

  • Tip 2. Take responsibility for confidence and develop strategies that will make confidence on competition day an expectation, not a hope.

  • Tip 3. There are many different types of confidence that underlie general confidence.

  • Tip 4. Analyze and figure out where your confidence comes from. Once these sources are understood, you will be able to easily control your powers and capabilities.

  • Tip 5. Whenever you strive to gain self-confidence, start with the little things and move up. This is achieved by identifying sources of confidence that will lead to self-confidence.
